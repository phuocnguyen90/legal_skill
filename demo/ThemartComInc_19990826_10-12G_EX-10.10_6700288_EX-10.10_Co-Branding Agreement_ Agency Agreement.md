## Negotiation Strategy & Prioritization

1. **Immediate “Deal‑Blocker” – Liability Cap**  
   * **Goal:** Insert a mutual cap (12 months of fees) and carve‑out for breach of confidentiality, IP infringement, fraud.  
   * **Leverage:** The parties are both technology/online service providers; unlimited liability is commercially unreasonable and could jeopardize insurance coverage.

2. **Indemnity Scope & Caps**  
   * **Goal:** Limit indemnity to IP infringement, data‑breach, and statutory violations; add a monetary cap (12 months of fees) and a duty to mitigate.  
   * **Fallback:** If the counter‑party resists caps, negotiate a **“materiality threshold”** (e.g., indemnity applies only for claims exceeding US $ 50,000).

3. **Data‑Protection Addendum**  
   * **Goal:** Attach a DPA that satisfies CCPA/California privacy law and sets a 72‑hour breach‑notification window.  
   * **Fallback:** If the counter‑party objects to a full DPA, at minimum secure a **Breach Notification Clause** and a data‑deletion obligation.

4. **Termination‑for‑Convenience**  
   * **Goal:** Add a mutual 30‑day notice right for convenience.  
   * **Fallback:** If resisted, propose a **“material change”** clause allowing termination for any change in business strategy, not just equity change.

5. **Advertising Fees Clarity**  
   * **Goal:** Define the calculation method, set a ceiling (e.g., 0.05 % of average transaction), and require written amendment for any change.  
   * **Fallback:** Accept the “good‑faith” language but request a **schedule** (e.g., quarterly review) and a **dispute escalation** path.

6. **Confirm Acceptance of IP Licensing**  
   * **Goal:** Obtain a written representation that any future sublicensing will be limited to “visibility” only and will not expand the scope of the license.  
   * **Fallback:** If the counter‑party wants broader sublicensing, negotiate a **right of first refusal** for the other party.
